Aaron Jezghani

I am currently the Scheduler Architect with the Partnership for an Advanced Computing Environment (PACE) at Georgia Tech. Previously, I was a member of the Outreach and Faculty Interaction team with PACE, having joined in 2019 after finishing my PhD at the University of Kentucky, where I conducted precision studies of free-neutron and nuclear beta decay.
I had considered trying to become an astronaut, but I exceeded the height requirement.

My main research interests include the integration of novel computing architectures into community-driven infrastructure such as scheduler and gateways, as well as orchestrating workflow through said infrastructure and systems-level functionality. I am also interested in architecture-specific algorithm optimization and green computing.
